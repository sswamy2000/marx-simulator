Each firm produces widgets as a direct function of workers, hours per day, days per week, and the tech multiplier. Firms are price takers. Market share is proportional to each firm's actual output. A firm whose cost per widget exceeds the market price cannot cover costs and exits production.

Built from Marx, Capital Vol. I (Ch. 10, 15) and Vol. III (Ch. 13–15).
Output = workers × hours/day × days/week × 52 × tech multiplier. Each labor-hour produces 1 widget at multiplier = 1.
Market share = firm output / total combined output (profitable firms only). Units sold = min(output, market size × share).
C (purchase) = mult × v. Annual depreciation = C / lifespan (longer lifespan → lower charge). C avg = C × (lifespan/2).
Annual cost = depreciation + v. Rate of profit = s / (C̅ + v) where C̅ = avg capital tied up — Vol. III formulation.
Firms are price takers. Cost/unit > market price = market exit. Unsold widgets: cost incurred, no revenue.
